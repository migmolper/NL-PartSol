Section 6.\+1 \+: Conjugate Gradient Method with Preconditioning One of the most effective and simple iterative methods (when used with preconditioning) for solving \$\+Ax = b\$ is the conjugate gradient algorithm. The algorithm is based on the idea that the solution \$\+Ax = b\$ minimizes the total potential \$\textbackslash{}\+Pi = \textbackslash{}frac\{1\}\{2\} x$^\wedge$T A x -\/ x$^\wedge$T b \$. Hence, the task in the iteration is, given an approximate \$x$^\wedge$k\$ to \$x\$ for wich the potential is \$\textbackslash{}\+Pi$^\wedge$k\$, to find an improved aproximation \$x$^\wedge$\{k+1\}\$ for wich \$\textbackslash{}\+Pi$^\wedge$\{k+1\}$<$\textbackslash{}\+Pi$^\wedge$k\$. Howerver, not only do we want the total potential to decrease each iteration but we also want the total potential to decrease in each iteration but we also want \$x$^\wedge$\{k+1\}\$ to be calculate efficiently and the decrease in the total potential to occur rapidly. Then the iteration will converge fast.

In the conjugate gradient method, we use in the kth iteration the linearly independent vectors \$p$^\wedge$1,p$^\wedge$2,p$^\wedge$3,\textbackslash{}ldots,p$^\wedge$k\$ and calculate the minimum of the potential in the space of the potential in the space spanned by these vectors. This gives \$x$^\wedge$\{k+1\}\$. Also, we stablish the additional basis vector \$p$^\wedge$\{k+1\}\$ used in the subsequent iteration.

The algorithm can be summarized as follows\+: \textbackslash{}begin\{enumerate\}

\textbackslash{}item Choose the iteration vector \$x$^\wedge$1\$ (frequently \$x$^\wedge$1\$ is the null vector).

\textbackslash{}item Calculate the residual \$r$^\wedge$1 = b -\/ Ax$^\wedge$1\$. If \$r$^\wedge$1 = 0\$, quit.

\textbackslash{}item Else \+: \textbackslash{}begin\{enumerate\} \textbackslash{}item \$p$^\wedge$1 = r$^\wedge$1\$ \textbackslash{}item Calculate for \$k = 1,2, \textbackslash{}ldots\$ \textbackslash{}begin\{equation\} \textbackslash{}alpha$^\wedge$k = \textbackslash{}frac\{r$^\wedge$\{k$^\wedge$T\} r$^\wedge$k\}\{p$^\wedge$\{k$^\wedge$T\} A p$^\wedge$k\} x$^\wedge$\{k+1\} = x$^\wedge$k + \textbackslash{}alpha$^\wedge$k p$^\wedge$k r$^\wedge$\{k+1\} = r$^\wedge$k -\/ \textbackslash{}alpha$^\wedge$k A p$^\wedge$k \textbackslash{}beta$^\wedge$k = \textbackslash{}frac\{r$^\wedge$\{k+1$^\wedge$T\} r$^\wedge$\{k+1\}\}\{r$^\wedge$\{k$^\wedge$T\} A r$^\wedge$k\} p$^\wedge$\{k+1\} = p$^\wedge$\{k+1\} + \textbackslash{}beta$^\wedge$k p$^\wedge$k \textbackslash{}end\{equation\} \textbackslash{}end\{enumerate\}
